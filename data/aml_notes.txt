AML Structuring:
Structuring is a money laundering technique where criminals break large financial transactions into many smaller ones to avoid regulatory reporting thresholds. For example, instead of depositing $20,000 at once, a person may deposit $4,000 five times.

Smurfing:
Smurfing is a form of structuring where multiple individuals or accounts are used to conduct small transactions to hide the origin of funds.

FATF Recommendation 16:
FATF Recommendation 16 focuses on wire transfers. It requires financial institutions to collect and transmit accurate information about the sender and recipient to prevent money laundering and terrorist financing.

Sanctions Screening:
Sanctions screening is the process of checking customers, transactions, and counterparties against international sanctions lists such as OFAC, UN, EU, and UK lists.

Suspicious Transaction:
A suspicious transaction is any financial activity that appears unusual, lacks a clear economic purpose, or may involve money laundering, fraud, or terrorism financing.

Red Flags in Financial Transactions:
- Repeated transactions just below reporting thresholds
- Sudden large transfers
- Frequent transfers between unrelated accounts
- Transactions involving high-risk countries
- Structuring or smurfing patterns
